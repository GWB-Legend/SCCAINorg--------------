Our Mission & Vision

SHOUT CLIMATE CHANGE AFRICA (SCCA) is a Non-Governmental, non-political, non-ethnical, non-racial Organization that encourages all to "SHOUT NOW OR NEVER" on climate change issues that may affect us all.

Founded in 2016, our organization works with communities, governments, and partners across Africa to implement sustainable solutions to climate change challenges.

We focus on advocacy, education, and practical projects that address environmental degradation and promote sustainable development.

Our History

Shout Climate Change Africa (SCCA) was founded in 2016 and registered under the Companies Act No 5 of 2009 with Company No SL10716SHOUT20491 by a small group of environmental activists residents in Freetown who came together to share strategies for tackling environmental problems which poses threat to the lives and properties of the country â€“ Sierra Leone, Africa and the rest of the world.

Shout Climate Change Africa (SCCA) is firmly dedicated to supporting the struggles of communities adversely affected by unsustainable exploitation of human and natural resources.

Campaign priorities for 2023 -2026 include transforming societies away from depositing refuse dumps in drainage, fossil fuels and towards decentralized, community-owned and controlled sustainable energy, exposing and stopping land grabs which occur when land used by local communities is leased or sold to foreign investors, including corporations and governments.

SHOUT NOW OR NEVER! on climate change issues that may affect us all, formed two years ago, is made up of scores of various professional experts that work with other Organizations, Communities, NGOs, Institutions, Companies, Governmental MDAs and schools; with the single aim of providing services that address climate change, environmental problems and related issues.

Since it started, SCCA has undertaken some environmental related assignments through working with local radios, televisions, newspapers and other organizations to jointly address community and environmental related concerns and problems. Key among them was transforming communities from depositing refuse dumps in drainage, support and encouraged communities to contribute and priorities tree planting and reforestation activities; renewable energy usage to fossil fuels and towards decentralized community-owned and controlled sustainable energy.

Shout Climate Change Africa is an independent organization that acts to change attitudes and behaviors of individuals in order to protect, conserve the environment and promote peaceful, healthy living.

Shout Climate Change Africa has a well-structured management system; it operates from a coordinating center in Freetown, with personnel in the Regional headquarters and hopes to have Focal Persons in all districts in Sierra Leone and Africa at large.

Over the years, the effect of climate change has greatly affected Sierra Leone. The rampant deforestation is very visible in the rate of reduction of the country's forests cover (forest trees) due to but not limited to the followings:

  1. The rampant and uncontrolled/unsustainable charcoal burning
  2. Illegal and rampant logging all over the country for industrial and domestic uses
  3. Illegal tree cutting for smoking fish, salt processing and for firewood and charcoal burning.
  4. Large scale Sand and mineral Mining activities clearing large tracts of forests land without compensatory reforestation programmes

All of the above have led to large exposed dry lands, large paved road surfaces and so greater runoff and massive erosion in some areas and longer dry spells. This situation nationwide is now unacceptable and cannot be allowed to continue unabated and unregulated.

SDG Goal 13 requires "Climate Action" since inaction will have a direct consequence on non-development of communities that are affected thereby encourage the circle of poverty.

To undertake "Climate Action" according to the Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 13 requires solving those problems that are posed by climate change.

The targeted beneficiaries are charcoal burners, farmers and the underprivileged, with focus on youth and women, with a total rippling effect (indirect beneficiaries) of some 5,000 communities and other members that depends on charcoal for the heating/cooling source of energy.

It will reduce the effect of climate change and improve the ecosystem/biodiversity as citizens negative activities on the environment will be controlled through greater awareness of citizens' roles and responsibilities on climate change and the environment.

This project hopes to assist in stopping further deforestation as well as assisting the people to be economically independent. Detailed of the full project document has been developed and will be made available to you if you are interested to partner with SCCA.

LAND RESTORATION

PROJECT 1: RESTORATION / RECLAMATION OF THE LAND LOST DUE TO COASTAL EROSION AND SEA LEVEL RISE FROM SUGAR LAND TO LAKKA IN THE WESTERN AREA OF SIERRA LEONE.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

SHOUT CLIMATE CHANGE AFRICA (SCCA) is a Non-Governmental, non-political, non-ethnical, non-racial Organization that encourages all to "SHOUT NOW OR NEVER" on climate change issues that may affect us all.

Over the years, the effect of climate change has greatly affected the coastal communities of Freetown especially the Goderich to Lakka areas. Sustainable Development Goal 13 requires "Climate Action" since inaction will have a direct consequence on non-development of communities that are affected thereby encouraging the circle of poverty.

To undertake "Climate Action" according to the Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 13 requires solving those problems that are posed by climate change.

Thus the coastal erosion in the above areas could not be dissociated from sea level rise.

In Sierra Leone especially Freetown coastal areas around the Goderich to Lakka, there is massive coastal erosion that is destroying valuable property like the St Michael Lodge and other important structures and the eventual loss of the beautiful beaches along those places. The direct effect is the loss of the country's tourism income generation that these beaches would have provided.

This project hopes to assist in stopping further "eating into the land by the sea" and totally reclaim the land and beaches lost in the above process; that will create a bigger problem if not checked by now. Detailed of the full project document has been developed and will be made available to you if you are interested to partner with SCCA.

TARGET BENEFICIARIES

The targeted beneficiaries are some 200,000 peninsular-mountain community people including some 150 villagers valley, vegetable farmers and the underprivileged, with focus on youth, women, children and as well as the physically challenged (Disabled) with a total rippling effect (indirect beneficiaries) of some 300,000 other community members.

It will reduce the effect of climate change and improve the ecosystem/biodiversity as citizens negative activities on the environment will be minimized through greater awareness of citizens' roles and responsibilities on climate change and the environment. It will also create employment for the youths of the communities, and they will claim ownership and protection for it when the beautiful landscape and biodiversity of these areas will be restored.
